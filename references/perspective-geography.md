# Perspective Geography: the map is a point of view

There is no neutral map. Every place name was given by *someone*, standing *somewhere*, in *some
era*, for *some reason*. A world that names places from a single god's-eye view feels like a board.
A world where the same place has different names depending on who you ask, and where those names
*change as power and knowledge change*, feels inhabited.

## The keystone idea: center and margin are relative

The canonical example: a creature drifts between the stars. Humans, standing on a planet, call it a
**stardust worm** — a worm, in the dust between worlds. But from the creature's frame, *the stardust
is the ocean*; it is a swimmer in an endless sea, and Earth is merely one more ordinary floating
**sky-island**, no more special than any other rock. Same universe, inverted figure and ground.

Apply this everywhere:
- What one people calls "the frontier / the edge / the wilds," its inhabitants call "home / the
  center of the world."
- What spacers call "down the well" (a planet) is, to planet-dwellers, simply "the world," and space
  is "the void / up there."
- A "dead zone" to one culture is "the quiet country" or "the ancestors' road" to another.

When you create a place, ask: **who is at its center, and who is naming it from outside?** Give at
least the insider name and the outsider name when both cultures matter.

## The POV name table (in each `04-geography/<place>.md`)

For any significant place, record how it's named across cultures and eras:

```
| Name | Used by | Era | Literal meaning / reason | Connotation |
|------|---------|-----|--------------------------|-------------|
| Stardust Sea | the drift-folk | all | "the ocean we live in" | home, total |
| The Dust | inner-system humans | Era 1-2 | empty space between bodies | barren, transit |
| The Vance Corridor | shipping cartels | Era 3 | first safe transit lane, eponym | commercial |
```

Also record, in the place file: its strategic/ecological role, what lives there, what *happened*
there (events tie to the timeline), and which names are currently ascendant vs. archaic.

## Names change with history

Power, knowledge, and catastrophe rewrite the map. Build this in:

- **Conquest & politics.** Victors rename. Constantinople → Istanbul; Saint Petersburg →
  Petrograd → Leningrad → Saint Petersburg. A station renamed three times tells you who held it.
- **New knowledge inverts framing.** Once people learn the "stardust worm" is sapient and the dust is
  its sea, educated humans start saying "the drift" instead of "the dust" — the *worldview shift
  shows up in vocabulary*. Old-timers keep the old word; that generational gap is texture.
- **Catastrophe renames.** A flooded city becomes "the Shallows." A reactor town becomes "the Glow."
  The new name embalms the disaster.
- **Mishearing & drift.** Names erode over centuries (see naming-provenance: loanwords/corruption).
  "New Amsterdam" → "New York"; let your places weather the same way.

When a historical leap occurs (see `emergent-history.md`), *go through the geography files and
rename* what the new era would rename. A redrawn map is one of the clearest signals of real change.

## Scale and the figure/ground flip

The same trick scales up and down, and is great for sci-fi vertigo:
- To a planet, a black hole is a monster; to a higher-dimensional being, it might be a *doorway* or
  even a *dwelling*.
- To humans, a Dyson swarm is a megastructure; to its builders, it's just "the lights" or "town."
- A "wormhole" (named by analogy to a worm boring through an apple — keep that etymology visible) is
  an exotic shortcut to outsiders and a daily commute to those who live by its mouth ("the throat").

Whenever you describe something awesome, ask who finds it *mundane*, and let them name it casually.
That casualness is what sells the scale.

## Checklist for a new place

- [ ] Who is at its center? Who names it from outside? Both names recorded?
- [ ] What does its name *literally mean*, and to whom?
- [ ] How would its name change after the next big historical turn?
- [ ] Is there a figure/ground flip available (someone for whom this awe is ordinary)?
- [ ] Linked to the events that happened there and the peoples who live there?
